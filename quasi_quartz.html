Quasi-Quartz & Related Terms

Quasi-Quartz – A rare crystalline mineral (SiO₂ with traces of aluminum and iron oxides) found only on the planet Agni. It stores and releases bioatomic energy efficiently and is vital to Neumin metabolism.

Quasisilicatalysis – The process by which Neumin extract energy from quasi-quartz liquid crystal solutions via the Quasisilicaureal gland.

Silicacrine Cycle – A monthly biological cleansing process in which residual quasi-quartz particles are expelled.

QQLS (Quasi-Quartz Liquid Serum) – An injectable fluid containing quasi-quartz nanocrystals used for energy enhancement.

Silicacrinins – Enzymes that break down or tag inert quasi-quartz residue for excretion.

Purge Vesicles – Microstructures that transport residue to excretion sites.

Quasinephrines – Hormones regulating renal participation in the purge.

CLI (Crystalline Load Index) – Metric used to determine proximity to triggering the Silicacrine Cycle.

Quasisilicaureal Gland (QSAG) – A gland in the inner ear essential to quasi-quartz metabolism; targeted by several disorders.

Related Conditions

Quasisilicatonia – A balanced metabolic state allowing normal quasi-quartz regulation.

Quasisilicaneplasia – A genetic absence of functional quasi-quartz receptors or metabolism machinery.

Quasisilicadysria – A condition of unstable or inconsistent quasi-quartz metabolization.

Quasisilicatropism – Enhanced resonance enabling passive energy absorption (quasisilicaptosis).

Quasisilicacarcinergy – Hypersensitive immune response causing allergic reactions to quasi-quartz.

Quasisilicaureitis – Autoimmune attack on the Quasisilicaureal gland leading to fatigue and metabolic issues.

QARS (Quasisilica Auto-rejection Syndrome) – Severe immunometabolic disorder where the body rejects quasi-quartz implants and internal stores.

QDLS (Quasisilicadermal Load Syndrome) – Dermatological condition with excess quasi-quartz accumulating in skin pores, forming painful lesions.
