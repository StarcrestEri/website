Timeline of the Surya System

8000 BNE

Living in an antimatter‑dominated universe, humanity flees a dying sun and settles in the Surya Star System among the stars of Andromeda, building the first orbital habitats.

6000 BNE

Primitive fusion technology is mastered; first contact with native microbial life in the system’s asteroid belt occurs.

4000 BNE

The Neumin species emerges through accelerated evolution, featuring elongated ears, internal bioatomic energy storage, and a dependency on quasi‑quartz crystals for power regulation.

3500 BNE

A technological revolution ushers in mass artificial incubation; natural birth becomes rare and sexual organs are deemed obsolete.

3000 BNE

Neumin society reorganizes around communal parenting; gender distinctions survive only in ceremonial rites.

2000 BNE

Political factions form between traditionalists and proponents of further bioengineering; exosuits are developed to augment innate abilities.

1500 BNE

The first cumbersome exosuits appear, allowing warriors to channel bioatomic energy into external weapons and shields.

500 BNE

A catastrophic accident at a quasi‑quartz incubation facility leads to widespread reform and the establishment of interplanetary safety codes.

50 BNE

The amphibious Kero emigrate from the frozen world Himavat to the swamp moon Tara, beginning their long coexistence with mainland villagers.

40–12 BNE

This quarter‑century sees the births of the future protagonists Rina, Sami, Alia, and Asha. The brilliant astronomer Niyo studies a nascent black hole at the system’s centre and begins sketching plans for energy harvesting.

8 BNE

Construction commences on an immense ring structure around the black hole to siphon its energy; early stages are heavily automated.

5–3 BNE

Niyo’s obsession deepens; his behaviour grows erratic. Sami vanishes during a reconnaissance of the black hole, which later becomes known as Shiva.

1 NE

Niyo stages a coup, overthrowing the Neumin High Council and proclaiming himself emperor. The Niyo Empire institutes harsh policies to fuel its expansion.

2 NE

Political undesirables and dissenters are deported to Himavat, forced to labour constructing the raw core of Shiva – the mechaplanet projected to encircle the ring.

4 NE

Scattered uprisings against imperial rule are quelled. Alia, now a young fighter, begins covert resistance work alongside her family.

5 NE

The events of Alia and the Lost Stars take place: Alia embarks on a perilous journey through the Dream Land to collect fragments of her memory and confront the mysterious forces manipulating the system. Her actions spark a renewed hope among the oppressed.

6 NE

Present day of the current story; the ring has solidified into a habitable mechaplanet and the empire’s grip remains tight, but whispers of rebellion grow louder.
