Neumin

Neumin are the descendants of humanity who adapted to life in the Surya System. They are slender, fast, and possess elongated ears. A bioatomic adaptation allows them to store trace amounts of energy from certain chemicals. To amplify this ability they rely on Quasi-Quartz crystals or injectable liquid serum.

Sexual reproduction was abandoned millennia ago. By 3500 BNE mass artificial incubation became the norm, and by 2000 BNE most Neumin were born sexless. Society still maintains gender rituals at birth and informal norms, but sexed individuals are extremely rare.

Their bodies undergo a monthly "Silicacrine Cycle" to purge residual Quasi-Quartz particles. A specialized organ, the Quasisilicaureal gland in the inner ear, handles this metabolism and is central to several medical conditions.

To channel their bioatomic power in combat Neumin developed exosuits. Early models were bulky and restrictive; by 1500 BNE these suits allowed greater energy storage at the cost of mobility. Over the centuries designs improved, becoming lighter and more agile, culminating in the agile suits seen in the days of Niyo.

Neumin society is shaped by their reproduction history. After mass incubation began in 3500 BNE and sexless births dominated by 2000 BNE, traditional sexual organs became vestigial. Gender gatekeeping persisted; parents assigned a gender at birth and could react angrily if offspring wished otherwise, though sexed individuals were so rare that most social structures catered to the sexless majority.

Major accidents such as the 500 BNE incubation facility disaster prompted tightening of laws and fostered a culture of caution around child-bearing technology. Despite their advanced biology, Neumin continue to rely on cultural rituals and hierarchical norms inherited from their human ancestors.
